About

At the intersection of molecular science, data, and public health

I'm a Mexican-born, Cambridge-based scientist with a trajectory that spans genomics, stem cell biology, organ-on-chip microphysiology, and translational medicine. My work has consistently sat at the boundary between laboratory science and its application — building the systems, assays, and analytical pipelines that connect fundamental biology to clinical and commercial impact. The arc has a deliberate shape: bioinformatics first, wet-lab second, and integrating them in all of my projects.

At CN-Bio, I worked as a Senior Scientist on organ-on-chip platforms coupling gut and liver for pharmaceutical ADME studies, operating in a hybrid R&D/CRO role that demanded both scientific rigour and client-facing delivery. At Sita Bio, I design endometrium-on-chip, directing the scientific strategy for modelling reproductive tissue in microphysiological systems. At bit.bio, I established the Translational Medicine laboratory from the ground up, bridging iPSC-derived cell products with their real-world therapeutic applications.

One of my current projects is on biosurveillance — specifically, whether discrete biological waste streams that are currently incinerated without analysis could be transformed into population-level epidemiological intelligence. It's a question that sits at the intersection of public health infrastructure, nucleic-acid analysis, and applied data science — and one I'm actively exploring with academic collaborators and potential co-founders.

Current Exploration

Biosurveillance from Untapped Specimen Streams

Investigating whether nucleic acids extractable from discrete solid sanitary and incontinence waste — currently incinerated without analysis — can power population-level disease surveillance inaccessible to existing systems. These specimens contain concentrated pathogen signals (STIs, antimicrobial resistance markers, viral genotypes) that are structurally absent from wastewater and clinical surveillance.

Key technical workstreams include multiplex qPCR assay design for a six-gene gonorrhoea AMR resistance panel, analytical validation in a novel specimen matrix, bioinformatics pipeline construction for population-level trend detection, and epidemiological modelling from pooled anonymous specimens.

Genomics & Bioinformatics

Sex-Specific Germline Transcriptomes in C. elegans

At the Kimble Lab (HHMI / University of Wisconsin–Madison), generated and analysed RNA-Seq datasets from isolated C. elegans gonads to map spermatogenic and oogenic transcriptomes. Identified 10,754 expressed mRNAs — doubling the number of germline-expressed genes catalogued in earlier studies. The paper (Ortiz et al., G3 2014; cited in Noble et al., Genetics 2016). The paper (Ortiz et al., G3 2014) ranks among the most-cited articles in G3, and the dataset (GEO: GSE57109) is widely used across the C. elegans community as a reference resource for defining oogenic and spermatogenic mRNA programmes.

Plant Molecular Biology

Plant microRNA Biology in Phaseolus vulgaris

As an undergraduate research intern at the Covarrubias Lab, Instituto de Biotecnología (IBT), UNAM, contributed to the characterisation of a second microRNA (miR159.2) encoded by the conserved plant miR159a precursor. Designed and executed reporter assays in Nicotiana benthamiana demonstrating that pvu-miR159.2 directs accurate cleavage of complementary target sequences at the canonical position between nucleotides 10 and 11 of the base-paired region — providing functional validation of the novel microRNA. Co-authored Contreras-Cubas et al., Plant Molecular Biology 2012.
