About

At the intersection of molecular science, data, and public health

I'm a Mexican-born, Cambridge-based scientist with a trajectory that spans genomics, stem cell biology, organ-on-chip microphysiology, and translational medicine. My work has consistently sat at the boundary between laboratory science and its application — building the systems, assays, and analytical pipelines that connect fundamental biology to clinical and commercial impact.

At CN-Bio, I worked as a Senior Scientist on dual organ-on-chip platforms coupling gut and liver for pharmaceutical ADME studies, operating in a hybrid R&D/CRO role that demanded both scientific rigour and client-facing delivery. At Sita Bio, I led the design of an endometrium-on-chip platform, directing the scientific strategy for modelling reproductive tissue in microphysiological systems. Then at bit.bio, I established the Translational Medicine laboratory from the ground up, bridging iPSC-derived cell products with their real-world therapeutic applications.

I'm currently focused on biosurveillance — specifically, whether discrete biological waste streams that are currently incinerated without analysis could be transformed into population-level epidemiological intelligence. It's a question that sits at the convergence of molecular diagnostics, data science, and public health infrastructure — and one I'm actively exploring with academic collaborators and potential co-founders.

Current Exploration

Biosurveillance from Untapped Specimen Streams

Investigating whether nucleic acids extractable from discrete solid sanitary and incontinence waste — currently incinerated without analysis — can power population-level disease surveillance inaccessible to existing systems. These specimens contain concentrated pathogen signals (STIs, antimicrobial resistance markers, viral genotypes) that are structurally absent from wastewater and clinical surveillance.

Key technical workstreams include multiplex qPCR assay design for a six-gene gonorrhoea AMR resistance panel, analytical validation in a novel specimen matrix, bioinformatics pipeline construction for population-level trend detection, and epidemiological modelling from pooled anonymous specimens.
